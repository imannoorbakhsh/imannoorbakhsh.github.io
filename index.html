about me

I did my bachelor of science in Computer Engineering (Information Technology) at the University of Tabriz. My area of expertise was security and computer maintenance. In my second semester, I took an internship at the Computer Emergency Response Team Laboratory of the University of Tabriz (APA). Every week, we reported directly to Dr. Mina Zolfy Lighvan, the laboratory founder and manager.

In the meantime, I started learning Kali Linux and watching cryptography courses from Ruhr University Bochum. In 2016, I decided to expand my knowledge in network engineering. I was able to get CCNA certification in both Routing & Switching and Datacenter.

In 2018, I got an offer to work with a professional team as a UI/UX designer. Our team worked directly under the supervision of Dr. Farnaz Derakhshan. We held weekly meetings to report about the assigned task to both Dr. Derakhshan and our project manager. It was at that time when I realized how much I am in love with this field.

Currently, I am planning to apply for human-computer interaction as my master's degree. For me, the beauty of this field is the multidisciplinary nature of HCI. It combines theories and practices from several fields, including computer science, cognitive science, anthropology, design, psychology, etc. Therefore, you need to master all these disciplines in order to be able to combine visual elements with computer science and human nature.
